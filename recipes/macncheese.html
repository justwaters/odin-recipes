Mac'n'cheese Recipe

Description

Homemade mac and cheese is a comforting dish that has the power to evoke nostalgia and warmth, making it a favorite for people of all ages. At its core, this beloved classic consists of tender pasta enveloped in a rich, creamy cheese sauce. The process begins by cooking elbow macaroni until al dente, while simultaneously preparing a béchamel sauce made from butter, flour, and milk. Once the sauce thickens, a generous amount of cheese—often a blend of sharp cheddar, mozzarella, and maybe a hint of Gruyère—is stirred in until it melts into a velvety mixture. The result is a luscious, cheesy coating that clings to each piece of pasta, creating a dish that is both indulgent and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated mozzarella cheese

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the macaroni according to the package instructions until al dente.
  3. Melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat.
  4. Add the flour and whisk until smooth, cooking for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Slowly pour in the milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps.
  6. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until it thickens, stirring occasionally.
  7. Remove from heat and stir in the cheddar and mozzarella cheese until melted and smooth.
  8. Combine the cooked macaroni and cheese sauce in a baking dish.
  9. Top with additional grated cheese if desired.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown and bubbly.
